## Content Insights
I learned that using a CMS doesn't completely eliminate the need for coding. Rather it shifts the focus toward managing template overrides. To maintain the specific aesthetic from my Figma prototype, I had to learn how to navigate the Joomla file structure to remove hardcoded page titles and adjust layout blocks that were restricted by default settings. <br>
The complexity of a CMS means that even simple visual changes often required a deep dive into the system's logic to find where a specific style is being generated. This reinforced the idea that "low-code" solutions still require a high level of technical skill to achieve a truly custom result.

## Career/Employability/Learning Insights
Transitioning from VS Code to Joomla provided a major insight into the compromise between granular control and scalability. While hardcoding in VS Code offers more creative freedom, it is incredibly high effort to scale. A CMS like Joomla was a major learning curve to overcome, but it is built for long-term sustainability and ease of content management. <br>
I've also realized that my learning is most effective when I treat AI as a technical peer. Instead of browsing generic forums, using AI for specific troubleshooting allowed me to bypass the CMS learning curve and get straight to the implementation. This learning strategy keeps me engaged because I am solving real world design problems as they arise, rather than just theorizing about them.
